About

Without dreams, we would never spread our wings. Without action, we would never learn to fly. Without school, we would never be able to share what gives us wings.

Who creates Early Stage?

Early Stage is an English language school that was created out of a passion for teaching, a need for development, and authentic contact with children and young people. School branches throughout Poland are created by people who hold these values close.
We cooperate with franchisees who create a space friendly for children's development. Our methodologists support teachers in improving their work and create ambitious educational materials. Teachers with passion, commitment and empathy conduct classes, focusing on authentic contact with students. We invite parents who care about the comprehensive development of their children, but above all children, whose colorful world drives us to action.

How did Early Stage come about?

It all started in Warsaw’s Mokotów district, in a grey, inconspicuous building, where Joanna Zarańska founded a language school, one of the first in Poland specializing in teaching English to children. After a few weeks, it turned out that the appearance of the building had nothing to do with what was happening inside. The classes were interesting, cheerful and colorful. Joanna’s enthusiasm combined with her own program implemented during the course gave amazing results. The young students talked about the lessons as if they were real adventures, full of puzzles, songs, rhymes and movement, increasingly using English words.

It was 1993, a supernova had just been discovered in the constellation of the Great Bear, and the Early Stage language school debuted in a small Warsaw housing e state, innovative in terms of teaching methods, opening children to the world, born under a lucky star.

Can you have two right hands?

It soon became clear that the school needed new people. But not ordinary English teachers, but passionate, creative, cheerful teachers who loved working with children and ambitious challenges. This is how Joanna's daughters joined the team: first Agnieszka, now known by her stage name Aga Zaryan, and after a few years Tonia, who currently manages the entire Early Stage company together with her husband Adam. For many years, the school operated as a small family business, gaining popularity and a wider group of new students year by year.

In 1997, it was time for a change. Joanna, at the urging of one of her parents, opened another branch of the school, in Ursynów. At Agnieszka's initiative, a branch was established in Warsaw's Ochota district. This is how Early Stage's journey ar ound the capital began. Today, our schools operate throughout Poland.

Small steps of big success

Our own textbooks, several years of experience, hundreds of ideas for interesting classes and enthusiasm were the potential with which the school entered the new millennium. The New Year's Eve countdown began, and with it the dynamic development of the company. Talented people joined the teaching staff, new projects and branches were created. In 2005, Early Stage was awarded the European Language Label for innovation in teaching a foreign language for the first time. In the years 2007-2011, the school went beyond the borders of Warsaw, appearing in the largest Polish cities. Currently, it operates in over 850 locations, teaching over 58,000 children.

Apart from numerous awards and educational successes, the greatest reason for pride are the young graduates of the school, leaving its walls with an excellent knowledge of English. Their openness to the world and wide development prospects are the best motivation for further work. Since the beginning of the school, children have inspired us the most.
